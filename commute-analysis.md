# Portland ↔ Beaverton Sustainable Commute Analysis

**Corridor:** Downtown Portland → Beaverton, OR  
**Distance:** ~12.8 km (8 miles)  
**Primary Route:** Sunset Highway (US-26) / Beaverton-Tigard Freeway  
**Data Sources:** OpenStreetMap, Plug Share, Super Charge Info  
**Last Updated:** July 2025

---

## EV Charging Infrastructure

### Portland Downtown Side (5 km radius)
- **11 stations** identified (6 with verified connector data)
- **Best DC Fast:** Tesla Supercharger at 805 SW Broadway (250 kW, 24/7, ~317 m)
- **Best multi-EV:** Multi-protocol station at ~644 m (J-1772 + CCS + CHAdeMO)
- **L2 Coverage:** Blink (3 stations) + ChargePoint dominate the 500–700 m zone
- **Gap:** No Electrify America or EVgo within 1 km downtown

### Beaverton Side (5 km radius)
- **5+ stations** identified
- **DC Fast options:** Electrify America (50–350 kW) and EVgo (50–200 kW) within 2 km
- **L2 options:** ChargePoint (Walmart), Blink downtown
- **Multi-protocol:** Station near SW Hall Blvd supports all major EVs

### Full Corridor Coverage
Combining both endpoints provides complete charging visibility for the round-trip commute:
- **Top-up strategy:** Level 2 overnight or at work (Beaverton) is sufficient for the 12.8 km daily commute (<4 kWh)
- **Fast-charge strategy:** Tesla Supercharger for quick Portland top-up; Electrify America/EVgo for non-Tesla detours

## Route Details

### EV Route
SW Broadway → SW Madison → Sunset Highway (US-26 West) → Beaverton-Tigard Freeway (US-26) → SW Beaverton Hillsdale Highway → SW Farmington Road → SW Hall Blvd → Beaverton workplace

### Bike Route (Surface Streets)
SW Madison St → SW 4th Ave → SW Main St → SW Broadway → SW Clay St → SW Sunset Blvd → Beaverton-Tigard Freeway bike path → SW Beaverton Hillsdale Hwy → SW Hall Blvd

⚠️ **Caution:** The Sunset Highway (US-26) is a busy 4-lane highway. Where possible, use protected bike lanes and the SW Corridor Greenway. The bike route is **marginally feasible but requires caution on Highway segments.**

### Transit Route
Portland Transit Mall → Blue Line (Metro) → Beaverton Transit Center → local bus/short walk to employment area. Total: ~35–50 min.

### Walk Route
⚠️ **Not recommended.** The commute passes through a highway corridor with no pedestrian infrastructure for most of the route. 2.5–3 hours of walking through high-speed traffic areas.

## Sustainability Recommendations

1. **For new EV commuters:** This corridor is an ideal test case. The existing Level 2 infrastructure at both endpoints makes daily charging trivial (overnight at home, or top-up at work).
2. **For multi-EV households:** The multi-protocol DC Fast stations on both sides mean any EV brand can complete the commute with confidence.
3. **For bike/walk advocates:** The Sunset Highway barrier is real. Supporting protected bike lanes and the SW Corridor Greenway extension would make cycling a genuine option.
4. **For policymakers:** Adding DC Fast chargers at both transit terminals (Portland Transit Mall & Beaverton TC) would support EV-transit hybrid commuting.
