# Bike and Walk Commute Analysis: Portland ↔ Beaverton

**Corridor:** Downtown Portland, OR → Beaverton, OR  
**Distance:** ~12.8 km (8 miles)  
**Data Sources:** OpenStreetMap, Plug Share, Super Charge Info  
**Last Updated:** July 2025

---

## Route Overview

The Portland ↔ Beaverton commute passes primarily along two major highways:
1. **Sunset Highway (US-26 West)** from downtown Portland
2. **Beaverton-Tigard Freeway (US-26)** through the Tualatin Valley to Beaverton

These highways create the backbone of the commute route but pose significant challenges for bike and foot travel.

### Key Route Segments

1. **Downtown Portland** (0–2 km): Ride SW Madison St and SW Broadway — well-signed bike routes through the city core.
2. **SW Clay Street to SW Sunset Blvd** (2–6 km): Surface street riding through the Goose Hollow neighborhood. Nice bike infrastructure on SW Sunset.
3. **Sunset Highway / US-26 crossing** (6–8 km): ⚠️ **Major barrier** — this is a busy 4-lane highway with fast-moving traffic. Use the **SW Corridor Greenway** for protected cycling where available.
4. **Tualatin Valley** (8–11 km): Follow the Beaverton-Tigard Freeway bike path along the freeway corridor.
5. **Beaverton** (11–12.8 km): Arrive via SW Beaverton Hillsdale Hwy → SW Farmington Rd → SW Hall Blvd to destination.

### Bike Infrastructure Along the Corridor

| Segment | Infrastructure | Quality |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| Downtown Portland | Bike lanes, lane markings | ✅ Good |
| Goose Hollow | Bike lanes on SW Sunset | ✅ Good |
| US-26 (Sunset Hwy) | No dedicated bike lanes | ❌ Poor — use greenway |
| Tualatin Valley | Multi-use path along freeway | ✅ Good |
| Beaverton | Mixed bike lanes and sharrows | ⚠️ Fair |

### Bike Commute Tips
- **Best time:** Weekday mornings before 7 AM and evenings after 6 PM avoid the worst highway merge traffic
- **Safety gear:** High-visibility clothing and front/rear lights are essential for the highway segments
- **Alternate route:** The **SW Corridor Greenway** provides a fully separated bicycle path for the most dangerous highway sections
- **E-bike recommended:** The 12.8 km distance with riding into a West Hills headwind makes an electric assist very valuable
- **Charge planning:** With an EV at both ends, a 30-minute bike commute requires no charging — but an e-bike would need a charge at work

## 🚶 Walk Commute Analysis

### Feasibility: ❌ Limited — Highway Corridor

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Distance** | 12.8 km (8 miles) |
| **Estimated Duration** | 2.5–3 hours |
| **Elev. Gain** | ~150 m |
| **Feasibility** | ❌ Unfeasible for daily commute |
| **CO₂ per Commute** | 0 kg |

### Why Walking Is Not Recommended

1. **No pedestrian infrastructure along US-26**: The Sunset Highway and Beaverton-Tigard Freeway segments have no sidewalks or crosswalks for most of their length.
2. **High-speed traffic**: Highway shoulders are designed for vehicles, not pedestrians. Crossings are infrequent and dangerous.
3. **Excessive time**: A 2.5–3 hour one-way walk is unsustainable for a daily commute.
4. **Weather exposure**: Much of the route lacks covered areas and Portland's rainy climate makes extended outdoor walking uncomfortable.

### Where Walking IS Feasible Within the Corridor

| Segment | Walkable? | Distance | Why |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----|
| Downtown Portland to Goose Hollow | ✅ | ~2 km | Urban neighborhood with sidewalks |
| Beaverton (walk to/from transit) | ✅ | ~1 km | Walkable downtown area |
| **General corridor** | ❌ | ~10 km | Highway with no pedestrian access |

### Better Alternative: Walk + Transit

The recommended sustainable option for non-riders:
1. **Walk** to a TriMet stop near downtown Portland (~5–10 min)
2. **Blue Line (MAX)** from Portland City Center to Beaverton Transit Center (~30 min)
3. **Walk or short bus ride** from Beaverton TC to your workplace (~5–10 min)
4. **Total:** 35–50 minutes, compared to 2.5+ hours walking the full route

## 📍 EV Charging for the Commuter

For EV commuters on this corridor:

| Strategy | Location | Cost/Effort |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Overnight home charging | Portland residential (Dayton, Goose Hollow, NW) | Standard L2, very cheap |
| Work top-up | Beaverton (Walmart, downtown, PGE) | Level 2, often free |
| Fast charge for detour | Tesla/Electrify America/EVgo on SW Beaverton Hillsdale Hwy | DC Fast, 15-30 min |

With ~4 kWh needed for the round-trip (12.8 km × 2), Level 2 charging at home or work easily covers the daily need.
