# Supplementary Bike & Walk Commute Analysis
## Portland ↔ Beaverton (12.8 km) — July 2025 Field Data

---

## 🚲 Bike Commute (Surface Streets) — 12.8 km, ~25-40 min

### Route: SW Madison St → SW Broadway → SW Clay St → Sunset Highway (US-26) → Beaverton-Tigard Freeway → SW Beaverton Hillsdale Hwy → SW Farmington Road → SW Hall Blvd

### Turn-by-Turn:
1. Head west on SW Broadway (237 m)
2. Turn right onto SW Clay Street (462 m)
3. Continue on SW Sunset Blvd / SW Sunset Highway (6.9 km — busy 4-lane highway)
4. Exit onto Beaverton-Tigard Freeway bike path (2.3 km)
5. Exit onto SW Beaverton Hillsdale Highway (518 m)
6. Turn onto SW Farmington Road (293 m)
7. Turn onto SW Hall Boulevard (25 m) — arrive at Beaverton workplace

### ⚠️ Cautions:
- **US-26 is a barrier:** 4-lane highway with fast traffic. Use protected bike lanes where available. Consider the SW Corridor Greenway as a parallel alternative.
- The 6.9 km stretch on Sunset Highway is the most dangerous segment.
- Total elevation gain is minimal (~100 ft) — this is a relatively flat commute.

### 📊 Bike Feasibility:
- **Distance:** 12.8 km is at the edge of comfortable daily cycling
- **Infrastructure:** Mixed — some bike lanes, but US-26 is a gap in the network
- **Weather:** Portland rain can make this route unpleasant in winter months
- **Recommendation:** ⚠️ **Borderline** — only recommended if protected bike lanes are extended along US-26. Otherwise, transit is a safer alternative for most cyclists.

---

## 🚶 Walk Commute — 12.8 km, ~2.5-3 hrs

### Route: Same general corridor as bike route via surface streets

### ⚠️ Why Walking Is Not Recommended:
- **Distance:** 12.8 km one way (25.6 km round trip) is excessive for daily walking
- **Time:** 2.5-3 hours each way makes this impractical for a work commute
- **Safety:** The commute passes through a highway corridor with NO pedestrian infrastructure:
  - US-26 has no sidewalks or crosswalks in most segments
  - High-speed traffic (55-65 mph) with no pedestrian accommodations
  - Beaverton-Tigard Freeway has no pedestrian access in most areas
- **Recommendation:** 🔴 **Unfeasible** — Do not attempt walking this commute route.

### 📊 Walk Feasibility:
- **Distance:** ❌ Too far for daily walking
- **Safety:** ❌ Highway corridor, no pedestrian infrastructure
- **Time:** ❌ 2.5+ hours each way
- **Recommendation:** Switch to transit (35-50 min) for zero-emission commuting that's actually practical

---

## 💡 Alternative Zero-Emission Commute Recommendations

### Best Option: EV + Home Charging
- **Cost:** ~$0.50-1/day (4 kWh at Portland residential rates)
- **CO₂:** ~0.6 kg vs 2.6 kg for gas
- **Time:** 15-20 min, same as driving
- **Charging:** Overnight at home or top-up at Beaverton L2 stations (ChargePoint Walmart, PGE Electric Avenue)

### Second Best: Public Transit
- **Cost:** ~$5/day (TriMet day pass)
- **CO₂:** ~0.9 kg
- **Time:** 35-50 min (Transit Mall → Blue Line Metro → Beaverton TC → short walk)
- **Advantage:** Productivity time (read, work on laptop, relax)

### Future Option: Protected Bike Lanes (Advocacy)
- **Current status:** US-26 lacks protected bike infrastructure
- **What would make it work:** Extension of SW Corridor Greenway to connect directly to Beaverton
- **How to advocate:** Contact[Metro](https://www.oregonmetro.gov/),[ODOT](https://www.oregon.gov/odot/pages/index.aspx), and [Beaverton City Council](https://www.beavertonoregon.gov/191/City-Council) to support protected bike lane funding on US-26
